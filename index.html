Axolotl

Scientific Name: Ambystoma Mexicanum

Conservation Status: Critically Endangered (Population decreasing)

The axolotl also known as a Mexican salamander or a Mexican walking fish, is a neotenic salamander, closely related to the tiger salamander. Although the axolotl is known as a "walking fish", it is not a fish, but an amphibian.

Balrog

Scientific Name: Balrogus

Conservation Status: ONLY ONE LEFT

Balrogs are described as tall and menacing with the ability to shroud themselves in fire, darkness, and shadow. They frequently appeared armed with fiery whips "of many thongs", and occasionally used long swords.

Mandarin Duck

Scientific Name: Aix Galericulata

Conservation Status: Population Decreasing

The mandarin duck, or just mandarin, is a perching duck species found in East Asia. It is medium-sized, at 41-49cm long with a 65-75cm wingspan. As the other member of the genus Aix, it is closely related to the North American wood duck.

Black Footed Ferret

Scientific Name: Mustela Nigripes

Conservation Status: Endangered

The black-footed ferret, also known as the American polecat or prairie dog hunter, is a species of mustelid native to central North America. It is listed as endangered by the IUCN, because of its very small and restricted populations.

West Indian Manatee

Scientific name: Trichechus manatus

Conservation Status: Endangered

The West Indian manatee or "sea cow" is a manatee, and the largest surviving member of the aquatic mammal order Sirenia. The West Indian manatee is a species distinct from the Amazonian manatee and the African manatee.

Pika

Scientific name: Ochotona

Conservation Status: Endangered

A pika is a small mammal, with short limbs, very round body, rounded ears, and no external tail. The name "pika" is used for any member of the Ochotonidae, a family within the order of lagomorphs, which also includes the Leporidae.

Black Rhino

Scientific name: Diceros bicornis

Conservation Status: Endangered

The black rhinoceros or hook-lipped rhinoceros is a species of rhinoceros, native to eastern and southern Africa including Botswana, Kenya, Malawi, Mozambique, Namibia, South Africa, Swaziland, Tanzania, Zambia, and Zimbabwe.

Sand Cat

Scientific name: Felis margarita

Conservation Status: Endangered

The sand cat, also known as the sand dune cat, is the only cat living foremost in true deserts. This small cat is widely distributed in the deserts of North Africa and Southwest and Central Asia.

Snow Leopard

Scientific name: Panthera Uncia

Conservation Status: Endangered

The snow leopard (Panthera uncia syn. Uncia uncia) is a large cat native to the mountain ranges of Central and South Asia. It is listed as endangered on the IUCN Red List of Threatened Species because, as of 2003, the size of the global population was estimated at 4,080-6,590 adults, of which fewer than 2,500 individuals may reproduce in the wild.

Woylie

Scientific name: Bettongia penicillata

Conservation Status: Endangered

The woylie, also known as the brush-tailed bettong, is an extremely rare small marsupial that belongs to the genus Bettongia. It is endemic to Australia. Formerly it had two separate subspecies, B. p. ogilbyi and the now extinct B. p. penicillata.
